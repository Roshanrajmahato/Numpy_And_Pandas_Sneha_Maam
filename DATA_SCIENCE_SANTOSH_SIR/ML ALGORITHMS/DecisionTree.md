# **Q24. Decision Tree**

## 🎤 How You Can Explain to Anyone (Natural Speaking Style)

**Decision Tree is a machine learning model that makes decisions step by step by asking simple Yes/No questions.**

You can imagine it like a **flowchart** or a **tree**, where:

* Each **question** is called a **node**
* Each **answer** creates a **branch**
* Final result is called a **leaf**

So basically, the model keeps asking questions until it reaches a final decision.

In simple words,
**Decision Tree splits the data into smaller parts using conditions until it makes a prediction.**

---

## 🌍 Real-Life Example (Very Easy to Explain)

**Loan Approval System 🏦**

Suppose a bank wants to decide whether to approve a loan.

The tree may work like this:

1. **Is income greater than ₹50,000?**

   * Yes → Next question
   * No → Reject loan

2. **Is credit score above 700?**

   * Yes → Approve loan
   * No → Reject loan

So step by step, it makes a **final decision**.

Just like humans make decisions by asking questions.

---

## 🧠 Even Simpler Way to Say

If interviewer wants short answer:

# 🔄 Cross Questions Interviewers May Ask

## 🔹 Cross Q1: Why are Decision Trees popular?

**Answer (Simple Style):**
Decision Trees are popular because they are **easy to understand and visualize**, and they work well even with **non-linear data**.

---

## 🔹 Cross Q2: What are the main parts of a Decision Tree?

**Answer:**

There are three main parts:

1. **Root Node** → First question
2. **Branches** → Outcomes of questions
3. **Leaf Nodes** → Final prediction

---

## 🔹 Cross Q3: What is overfitting in Decision Trees?

**Answer:**
Overfitting happens when the tree becomes **too deep** and learns noise instead of patterns.

So it performs very well on training data but poorly on new data.

---

## 🔹 Cross Q4: How can we prevent overfitting in Decision Trees?

**Answer:**

We can control:

* Maximum tree depth
* Minimum samples per split
* Pruning the tree

These help keep the tree simple.

---

## 🔹 Cross Q5: Can Decision Trees handle both classification and regression?

**Answer:**
Yes, Decision Trees can be used for:

* **Classification** → Predict category
* **Regression** → Predict numbers
